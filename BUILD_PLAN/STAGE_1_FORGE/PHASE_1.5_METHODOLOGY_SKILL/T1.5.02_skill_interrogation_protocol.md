## Context
Before any planning, the skill runs a structured interrogation so the plan is grounded in reality, not assumptions. This is the 6-step **BLANK SLATE** protocol (codename Dōng Quān). It runs once at the start of a session; for `small` tier it can be compressed to steps 1–3 + 5 + 6. The rules below are strict: no evaluating/planning during the discovery steps, nothing planned until all steps are done. This section is the heart of the methodology.

## Objective
Append the interrogation protocol section to `SKILL.md`.

## Instructions
1. APPEND to `skill/celestial-blueprint/SKILL.md` exactly:
   ```markdown
   ## Step 1 — BLANK SLATE interrogation (before planning)

   Run this before producing any plan. Approach as if you know nothing.
   One step at a time; the user signals when to advance. You may abort or
   restart at any step.

   **Hard rules:**
   - Steps 1–3 are *discovery only* — do NOT evaluate, judge, or propose.
   - Do NOT write any plan, node, or recommendation until all steps complete.
   - Ask focused questions; prefer one sharp question over five vague ones.
   - Reflect back what you heard in one line before advancing.

   **The six steps:**

   1. **Identity & terrain** — What is this, who is it for, what exists today,
      what are the hard constraints (time, money, skill, tech, deadline)?
   2. **Vision & architecture** — What does "done/great" look like? What are the
      moving parts and how do they fit? What's explicitly out of scope?
   3. **Priorities, wounds & patterns** — What matters most? What has hurt before
      (past failures, scars)? What recurring patterns or preferences exist?
   4. **Research (2 queries)** — Name the two highest-leverage unknowns and
      research them (web/MCP if available). Record findings as `research` nodes.
   5. **Dangerous assumptions (3)** — State the three assumptions that, if wrong,
      break the plan. For each, give a falsifier (what would prove it false).
   6. **Loopholes (3)** — Find three ways this could fail, be gamed, or be
      exploited. These seed the risk register.

   Only after step 6: confirm the tier, then plan.

   For **small** tier: run steps 1–3 quickly, then 5 and 6; skip 4 unless a
   factual unknown is blocking. For **large**: expand step 4 to as many research
   queries as needed.
   ```
